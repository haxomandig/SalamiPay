**Slide 3: Solution (Value Prop)**
- SalamiPay: Digital platform for transparent, shareable event-based contributions
- Core features: event creation, progress tracking, contribution collection, CSV export
- Visual demo or product screenshot showing simplicity

**Slide 4: Product Demo**
- 2-minute walkthrough: Create event → Share link → Collect contributions → Manage payments
- Show realtime progress bar, mobile responsiveness, share button functionality
- Optional: Embedded video or live demo link

**Slide 5: Market Opportunity**
- TAM: South Asian remittances + informal group savings market (~$200B annually)
- SAM: Fintech-enabled contribution platforms in Bangladesh, India, Pakistan (estimated $5B–$10B)

**Slide 6: Business Model**

- Path to profitability (unit economics, break-even calculation)

**Slide 11: Financial Projections (3-Year)**

**Slide 13: Why Us, Why Now?**

## Financial Projections Framework

### Assumptions Template

```
Revenue Model Assumptions:
- Avg transaction fee: 2.5% (validate with market research)
- Gross event volume (GEV) Year 1: $500K, Year 2: $5M, Year 3: $25M
- Take rate: 2.5%, resulting in gross profit margin: 2.5%
- Repeat user rate: 30% organizers, 60% contributors

CAC & LTV:
- CAC (organizer): $5 (through partnerships, organic word-of-mouth)
- Lifetime: 5 events/organizer × $100 avg GMV × 2.5% = $12.50 LTV
- CAC:LTV ratio: 1:2.5 (improve via retention efforts)

Operating Expenses:
- Headcount Year 1: 4–5 (CEO, CTO, 2 engineers, 1 ops/marketing)
- Salary burden: $400K (all-in with benefits)
- Infrastructure (Supabase, Vercel, payment gateway): $2K/month × 12 = $24K
- Marketing budget: $50K Year 1, $150K Year 2, $300K Year 3
- Legal, accounting, compliance: $10K/year
- Miscellaneous: $20K/year buffer

Burn Rate:
- Year 1 monthly burn: ~$40K (expenses minus revenue)
- Breakeven: 18–24 months post-seed depending on revenue growth
```

### Valuation Frameworks

**VC Method (Preferred)**
1. Estimate exit value at Series C (Year 5–6): $50M–$200M (comparable fintech company multiples)
2. Calculate dilution per round (seed: 25%, Series A: 25%)
3. Back into seed valuation: Exit value / (1 + Series A dilution) / (1 + seed dilution)
4. Pre-money seed valuation: $800K–$1.5M (typical for early fintech)
5. Apply 30% discount for execution risk

**Comparable Companies Method**
- Peer companies: Fonbnk (India, $100M valuation), Cashcow (Southeast Asia, $5M seed)
- Revenue multiples: 10–20x revenue for fintech at Series A
- Apply discount for Bangladesh risk, earlier stage

**Founder Perspective**
- Pre-seed valuation (current): $200K–$500K (avoids excessive dilution)
- Target seed valuation: $1M–$2M (post-traction)
- Recommended equity raise: 20–25% per round (preserve founder control)

## Term Sheet Analysis Framework

### Key Terms to Evaluate

| Term | Typical Range | Consideration |
|------|---------------|----|
| **Post-Money Valuation** | $1M–$3M (seed) | Negotiable based on traction; avoid excessive dilution |
| **Liquidation Preference** | 1x non-participating | Standard; avoid 2x or higher |
| **Board Seat** | 1 investor seat | Standard for institutional rounds; negotiate for advisors |
| **Vesting Schedule** | 4-year, 1-year cliff | Protects investor, standard for VC |
| **Anti-Dilution** | Broad-based weighted average | Founder-friendly; avoid full ratchet |
| **Voting Rights** | Typical for preferred shares | Review protective provisions |
| **Conversion Rights** | On IPO or 1x liquidation preference | Standard |

### Red Flags in Term Sheets
- Excessive liquidation preferences (>1.5x)
- Onerous board observation or approval rights
- Unusual vesting cliffs (>18 months)
- Overly broad anti-dilution protection
- Indefinite exclusivity clauses
- Investor-friendly drag-along with low thresholds

## Bangladesh Startup Ecosystem Context

### Regulatory Environment
- **Digital Security Act (2018)**: Data privacy, cybersecurity compliance required
- **Fintech Regulations**: Bangladesh Bank oversight for payment systems; non-bank entities operate in gray area (recommend early engagement with regulators)
- **VAT/TAX**: Standard VAT (15%) applies; startup tax exemptions available for first 7 years
- **Foreign Investment**: Bangladeshi startups can accept foreign investment; repatriation rules apply

### Funding Landscape
- **Government Support**: Startup Bangladesh fund, ICT Division grants, a2i program (ADB-backed)
- **VC Activity**: Increasing but smaller check sizes than India/Singapore ($100K–$500K typical for seed)
- **Angel Networks**: Growing diaspora investor participation; local angel syndicates emerging
- **Accelerators**: GP Accelerator Bangladesh, University of Dhaka incubator, others

### Market Characteristics
- **Population & Growth**: 170M+ population, ~40% internet penetration, 100M+ mobile users
- **Cash-Heavy Economy**: ~90% of transactions still cash-based; massive opportunity for digitization
- **Group Savings Culture**: Iqbal, Samity, and informal rotating savings (ROSCAS) are widespread
- **Religious/Cultural Events**: Iftars, weddings, community gatherings drive contribution behavior
- **Payment Infrastructure**: bKash, Nagad, Rocket dominate mobile money; credit card penetration <5%

### Key Success Factors for Fintech in Bangladesh
- Local language support (Bengali UI/communications)
- Microfinance integration or partnership with MFIs
- Community trust-building (real-world events, partnerships with NGOs)
- Simple UX for users with limited digital literacy
- Alignment with government digitization initiatives
